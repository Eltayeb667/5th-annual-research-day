Visual Impairment – Vision Measurement

What do you know about learners with SEN, specifically HI and VI?

Slide 1

  • Vision is often described using the Snellen fraction (e.g., 6/6 or 20/20).

  • 6/6 (20/20) = typical “normal” visual acuity.

  • 6/4 = better than average acuity: you can see at 6 m what typical vision sees at 4 m.

  • 6/12 = reduced acuity: you must be at 6 m to see what typical vision can see at 12 m.

Understanding Vision Ratios

Visual Acuity is expressed as a ratio. Explain the ratio to the audience.

Slide 2

  1. The first number = the test distance (often 6 meters; in the U.S. often 20 feet).

  2. The second number = the distance at which a person with typical vision can read the same line.

  3. LP = Light Perception.

  4. NLP = No Light Perception.

Hearing Impairment Measurement

How is hearing impairment measured?

Slide 3

  • Hearing level is often described in decibels (dB HL) using audiometry.

  • 0–20 dB = typical hearing range

  • 21–40 dB = mild hearing loss

  • 41–70 dB = moderate hearing loss

  • 71–90 dB = severe hearing loss

  • 91+ dB = profound hearing loss

SEN - Diversity

How diverse is this group?

Slide 4

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD): may affect communication and social interaction.

  • Attention-Deficit / Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD): inattention and/or hyperactivity‑impulsivity.

  • Specific Learning Disability: difficulties in reading, writing, or mathematics (varies by learner).

  • Down syndrome (Trisomy 21): genetic condition that may affect physical growth and intellectual development.

HTML5 & ARIA

ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) can enhance web accessibility for screen reader users. Key benefits include:

Slide 7

  1. ARIA can provide an accessible name/label when visible text is not enough.

  2. ARIA can specify roles, states, and properties (e.g., expanded/collapsed) for UI components.

  3. For dynamic updates, use patterns such as ARIA live regions to announce changes.
